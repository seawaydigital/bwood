About

Background &
Expertise

Dr. Brianne Wood is the inaugural joint Social Accountability and Learning Health Systems Associate Scientist in a tripartite partnership between NOSM University, Thunder Bay Regional Health Sciences Centre, and the Thunder Bay Regional Health Research Institute.

She holds a PhD in Epidemiology from the University of Ottawa and serves as Research Director of the NORTHH practice-based research network at the Dr. Gilles Arcand Centre for Health Equity. Her research bridges health systems science, social accountability, and primary care with a sustained commitment to Northern, rural, and remote communities.

In 2021, she was selected as a Health Systems Resilience Fellow by the Health Research Foundation and Innovative Medicines Canada — national recognition of her contributions to health system strengthening.

Research Focus
◈
Learning Health Systems

Development, implementation, and evaluation of integrated learning health systems in Northern Ontario, bridging education and health services research.

◇
Social Accountability

Frameworks and practice for socially accountable medical and health professional education, with implementation focus in Northern Ontario.

○
Community-Based Research

Participatory, collaborative research that brings together researchers, clinicians, communities, and policy-makers from the outset.

△
Rural & Remote Health Equity

Primary care capacity, health equity impact assessment, and health system performance in northern, rural, and remote settings.

"My vision is to improve health and health care in Northern, rural, and remote communities. Partnerships, shared strategies and resources between education, research, health services and communities are essential to create meaningful impact."

— Dr. Brianne Wood, 2021
